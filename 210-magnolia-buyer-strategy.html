Negotiation prep
Talking points that matter
These points are designed to help you understand the market clearly and approach the offer with confidence — not to talk you out of this home, but to make sure you go in with data on your side and an offer price the loan will support.
1
Comps
Smallest home in its comp set — priced at the highest $/sqft
Every closed comparable is larger, most by 500–800 sqft. Those homes averaged $134/sqft. At $169/sqft, the seller is asking a 26% premium per square foot over what similar homes have actually sold for. That gap is what your offer needs to address.
2
Zestimate
Zillow values this home at $259,700 — and its model is well-calibrated here
Zillow was within 1% on nearly every closed comp in this neighborhood. When the model is that accurate on the comps and still shows a 3.8% discount on the subject, that's a meaningful signal — not just an algorithm quirk.
3
Appraisal risk
On a USDA loan, the property must appraise — and this price is a real risk
An appraiser will see $134/sqft average on larger homes and have difficulty justifying $169/sqft on the smallest home in the set. If the appraisal comes in short, the deal either renegotiates or falls apart. Writing at a price the appraisal can support protects you from that scenario entirely.
4
Closing costs
Seller concessions reduce what the seller actually nets
Your $6,000–$8,000 in USDA closing costs and prepaids must be covered. Those come directly out of the seller's proceeds: offer price minus concessions equals net to seller. At $263K minus $7K concessions, the seller nets $256K — in line with what comparable homes have closed for.
5
Property
New roof and HVAC are maintenance items, not value-add upgrades
Real money spent, and they matter for condition. But appraisers treat these as preventing value loss — not creating a premium above market. The shop is genuinely rare and works in your favor. The outdated kitchen, baths, and very old appliances are the counterweight the appraiser will weigh against it.
6
Market context
Thin inventory is real — but the appraisal enforces value regardless
You've toured three rough properties in Barnett Bend. This home's condition is genuinely better. But a USDA loan cannot exceed appraised value. The data, not emotional scarcity, must anchor the offer. Limited options are leverage — not a reason to overpay.
